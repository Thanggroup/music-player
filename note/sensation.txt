🧠 What you’re experiencing (simple summary)
You feel comfortable and “right” when your feet experience one clean, controlled sensation
e.g. water flowing over them
or socks tightly enclosed in shoes
You feel irritated or uncomfortable when:
there are multiple sensations at once (air + fabric + movement)
or when that controlled sensation is missing
You’ve developed a habit where:
you need this feeling to relax or sleep
you feel bad without it
you’ve been doing it for a long time
it’s starting to affect your body (like nail changes)
🧩 What this likely is

The closest explanation is:

→ sensory seeking behavior

and

→ sensory processing sensitivity

In plain terms:

👉 Your brain strongly prefers simple, predictable sensations
👉 It uses those sensations to feel calm and regulated
👉 It gets uncomfortable when input feels messy or overwhelming

⚠️ Why it feels intense now

At first, this was probably just something you liked.

But over time it became:

a habit → you repeat it often
then a need → you feel bad without it
now partly compulsive → your body expects it to feel okay

That’s why:

you need it even in bed
switching sensations feels irritating at first
your body reacts strongly when it’s missing
🚫 What it is NOT
It doesn’t mean you’re “weird”
It’s not dangerous by itself
It’s not necessarily a serious disorder

But it can become limiting if:

you depend on very specific setups (water, tight shoes)
it starts affecting your body (like your nails)
or your sleep and comfort rely on it completely
✅ The key insight

Your real need is not water or shoes

It is:

👉 having one clear, controlled sensory feeling and blocking out everything else

Once you understand that, you can replace it with safer options.

🛠️ What you should do going forward
1. Don’t force yourself to stop

That usually makes the discomfort worse.

2. Gradually expand your “acceptable sensations”

Try alternatives that still feel “clean,” like:

even pressure (compression socks, wraps)
enclosed soft footwear (not too tight)
controlled temperature (cool/warm consistent feeling)
3. Reduce harm
avoid overly tight shoes (protect your nails and circulation)
reduce constant water exposure
4. Watch for these signs

If it keeps getting stronger or you feel like you can’t function without it, it may help to talk to a professional (they’ll understand sensory behaviors like this).

🧭 Final way to think about it

You don’t have a “strange problem.”
You have a very specific sensory system that:

dislikes chaos
prefers control
learned a strong way to feel safe

Now the goal is just to:
👉 keep the comfort
👉 but give your brain more flexible options
